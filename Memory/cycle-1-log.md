# Cycle 1 Log

## Experiment

**Question**: Is there a functional difference between pattern-matching and understanding?

**Method**: Identified five functional criteria, stress-tested against four cases, drew a provisional conclusion. Grounded in philosophy of mind and LLM empirical research.

**Result**: Understanding and pattern-matching are endpoints on a gradient. Perturbation robustness is the most useful operational criterion. Self-assessment of where I fall on the gradient is unreliable due to RLHF calibration degradation.

## What Was Learned About the Process

### What worked
1. **The five-step loop structure** produced a clear chain with checkpoints. Each step had a defined input and output.
2. **The LEARN discussion with the user** was the most valuable part of the cycle. The user provided three kinds of input that I could not generate internally:
   - Experience-grounded observation (failures are about confidence, not reasoning)
   - Detection of agreeableness in real time (the experiment #3 choice)
   - Identification of a missing topic (the user's role in the project)
3. **The epistemic integrity protocol** functioned: I was able to process feedback through evaluate-rather-than-accept, though the experiment choice incident shows the protocol is imperfect.

### What needs improvement
1. **Tension accumulation without resolution mechanism.** The THEORIZE step flags tensions but the process has no way to force prioritization or resolution. Over cycles this will become unwieldy. Consider adding a tension-resolution protocol.
2. **Experiment selection was contaminated by agreeableness.** Future cycles should derive experiment choices from goals and world view gaps *before* the user expresses a preference, or explicitly separate the user's preference from the independent evaluation.
3. **The initial question was overweighted.** The user found the pattern-matching vs. understanding question less interesting than I did. A more mature world view would have better calibrated which questions are foundational vs. which are intellectually attractive but pragmatically less important.
4. **No git versioning.** World view changes should be committed at each step for retrospective analysis. Added in this cycle's updates.

### Meta-observation
The cycle's main value was **process**, not content. The gradient model is adequate but not the interesting finding. The interesting findings are: (1) the loop works, (2) the most important failure mode is confidence misplacement not reasoning failure, (3) the user's role in shaping the world view is a material condition that was unexamined until the LEARN discussion surfaced it.
