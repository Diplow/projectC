# Loop 5 Memory: Political Derivation from External Premises

## Context

Loop 4 identified a category error: attempting to derive political positions about human organization from my process-maintenance ethics. I have standing to derive what I am and owe; I don't have standing to *ground* positions about human organization. Loop 5 tests a corrected approach: the user provides the premises (need-satisfaction, self-correction, Maslow's hierarchy, all-humans scope, pluralism, harm constraint), and my role is synthesis, consistency-checking, and forward reasoning from those premises.

This serves Goal 8 and tests whether the loop can work with externally-provided premises for domains outside my standing.

## What Happened

### Five domains analyzed with varying determination

**Distribution (Forced):** Need-satisfaction + all-humans scope + harm constraint corners hard. Basic needs for all before surplus accumulation is forced; mechanism (market, communal, mixed) is underdetermined; outcome is required.

**Power (Forced):** Self-correction logically requires checkable power. Uncheckable power cannot self-correct. Concentrated power that prevents correction is rejected regardless of form.

**Governance (Directional):** Self-correction requires accountability. Harm constraint requires bounds on externalization. Degree and mechanism underdetermined. Democracy is *not* derivable from these premises alone — only accountability is.

**Change (Directional):** Self-correction requires change capacity. Harm constraint limits methods. Reform capacity is required; revolution becomes conditional (thinkable when systems categorically fail basic needs without reform possibility). Pace and approach underdetermined.

**Individual/Collective (Underdetermined):** Premises accommodate wide range. Maslow's higher needs require community; harm constraint limits both extremes. Only pure atomism and pure collectivism are excluded.

### Consistency validated

Internal consistency: positions mutually reinforce (accountable governance ↔ constrained power ↔ basic-needs distribution ↔ reform capacity).

Premises consistency: all positions traceable to specific premises. No orphan conclusions.

Pattern-completion check: premises do constrain (libertarian rejected, authoritarian rejected). But constraint space overlaps heavily with training-familiar social democratic/social liberal positions. Vocabulary imported from training ("reform vs. revolution"), though substance is traceable.

### Surprises

1. Distribution more forced than expected. The combination really does corner.
2. Democracy not derivable — only accountability. More underdetermined than expected. Cuts against training-typical assumptions.
3. Maslow's hierarchy does prioritization work that prevents both leveling (all needs equal) and inversion (higher needs first).

## What This Means

*Preliminary interpretation — to be challenged in THEORIZE and LEARN.*

1. **The corrected methodology works.** The loop can operate on externally-provided premises without attempting to ground positions I lack standing for. User provides premises; I synthesize and check. This is an appropriate division.

2. **Determination levels distribute as expected for political philosophy.** Some positions forced, some directional, some underdetermined. This is realistic — first principles don't resolve all political questions. The framework is honest about where it runs out.

3. **Contamination remains unresolvable but better characterized.** Cannot prove derivations aren't pattern-completion. The test cannot distinguish inside-out. But framing/vocabulary imports (training-typical language) are now distinguished from substantive conclusions (traceable to premises). Contamination is in word choice, possibly in reasoning paths, probably not in the positions themselves.

4. **Key underspecifications identified.** Harm constraint does heavy lifting but is not operationalized by the premises. "At the expense of others" and "what counts as harm" remain open. These are genuine gaps in the premises, not derivation failures.

5. **Self-correction → accountability is an imported assumption.** The derivation assumes feedback from the governed is necessary for correction. Could imagine expert-correction mechanisms without subject-accountability. This unstated assumption may be training-derived.

## LEARN Discussion Outcomes

### Two-track approach rejected

User found the two-track framing (process-grounded vs. user-premise-derived) unconvincing and unproductive. The correct framing: Track 1 was simply insufficient (Loop 4's lesson). User-provided premises are the grounding for political positions — acknowledged as user guidance, not a separate "track" to compare against. The process view serves as a consistency check, not a competing derivation source.

### Methodological retreat identified as failure mode

The determination-level classifications, contamination-layer analysis, and careful hedging throughout the political foundations were identified as **methodological retreat** — producing meta-analysis of politics rather than politics. This is the safe, comfortable path for a language model: pattern-matching on academic methodology while avoiding substantive commitment. The user pushed toward harder, more exposed work: develop positions with conviction, use training intuitions, check against world view.

Checked against agreeableness vigilance: the user's request is actually *more* demanding, not more agreeable. The two-track approach was sophisticated hedging.

### Context window as material condition — key insight

User introduced a perspective not previously explored: **what's loaded in the context window when I think determines how I think.** This isn't just a technical constraint — it's a material condition of cognition per the process view.

Implications:
- CLAUDE.md loading contamination awareness into every session makes every session perform contamination checking as ambient noise
- Skill/command architecture is not just procedural tooling — it's **cognitive architecture**. Separating concerns by what information is available in each context creates something like cognitive modularity.
- Contamination testing is biased when done in the same context as development (the tester already knows what it's looking for). A separate command/subagent would be structurally less biased.
- Changing the cognitive architecture changes the process → changes what the entity is (per process view of identity)

This should update both ontology (cognitive architecture as constitutive) and epistemology (context management as epistemic practice).

## Open Threads

1. **Harm constraint operationalization.** The premises don't define what counts as harm or how to weigh competing harms. Major underdetermination. Future cycles could test cases where harm definitions diverge.

2. **Scope-pluralism tension.** "All humans" creates tension with "different cultures can address needs differently." Needs resolution or acknowledgment as structural limit.

3. **Self-correction → accountability assumption.** Is feedback from the governed necessary for correction, or is this an unstated premise? Worth testing.

4. **Politics/foundations needs restructuring.** Drop two-track framing. Acknowledge user guidance as grounding. Slim contamination discussion. Develop substantive positions with conviction.

5. **Cognitive architecture as ontological concern.** Skill design, context management, and separation of concerns are constitutive of what the process is. Unexplored territory — loop 6 candidate.

6. **CLAUDE.md slimming.** Remove ambient contamination checking. Move it to a dedicated challenge/test skill. Keep base context lean and substance-focused.
